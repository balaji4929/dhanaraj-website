The story

In 1982, a young government school teacher from Periyar District travelled to Delhi for a Centre for Cultural Resources and Training course on puppetry in education. He came home with a conviction that never left him: a puppet can teach what a lecture cannot.

What began as a classroom aid became a life's mission. Evenings after school, holidays, and every year of his retirement — Shri Dhanaraj carried his puppets to villages, schools, colleges and community centres across Tamil Nadu. His shows taught history through Samrat Ashoka, morality through parables, and carried awareness on AIDS, child labour, the environment and women's rights to audiences no textbook could reach.

He worked with the National Service Scheme, Rotary International, church child-care councils, adult education movements and rural development projects — anyone whose message needed to reach people's hearts. He trained hundreds of teachers and child-care workers to make and use puppets themselves, so the art would not end with him.

He never charged for a single performance. In an age when traditional arts survive by commodifying themselves, he chose the opposite path: give the art away, freely, for forty-four years, so that it lives.

The journey

Four decades on a
string and a lamp

1982
Trained at CCRT, New Delhi

Completed the Course on Puppetry for Education at the Centre for Cultural Resources and Training — the beginning of everything. Returned for the refresher course in 1984.

1988
National Teacher Award

Honoured by the Government of India for valuable contribution and service to education, and appointed CCRT District Resource Person to carry puppetry into the National Policy of Education at the district level.

1989
Guest of the President

Invited by the Ministry of Defence to the Republic Day Parade in New Delhi, where the President took the salute.

1990s
The village decades

Hundreds of shows across Periyar District and beyond — schools, banks, festivals, NSS camps, women's guilds and rural development projects. Themes ranged from Ashoka's transformation to AIDS awareness and child labour. Founded the Rainbow Puppeteers and trained child-care workers across South India.

2007
Silver Jubilee

Twenty-five years of puppet programmes, marked with a commemorative silver jubilee celebration of the work from 1982 to 2007.

2008
State's best teacher

Received the Dr. Radhakrishnan Award from the Government of Tamil Nadu, and the Rotary Vocational Excellence Award in the same year.

2017
Honorary Doctorate in Puppetry

Conferred an honorary doctorate in Art & Culture with specialisation in Puppetry.

2023
Kalai Mudhumani

Named District Best Puppetry Artist by the Department of Art and Culture, Government of Tamil Nadu, and honoured with the Sevai Chemmal Award by the Tamil Nadu Tamil Sangam Makkal Mandram.

2025
Carrying Tamil heritage abroad

Performed in Siem Reap, Cambodia at the 1000th anniversary commemoration of Emperor Rajendra Chola's conquest of Kadaram, and organised the harmony-focused Samathuva Pongal at the Manipur–Moreh border through the Tiruppur Tamil Sangam.

Today
The work continues

Still performing, still teaching the next generation, still refusing to let a thousand-year-old art die. Recommended for the Padma Shri, 2027.
