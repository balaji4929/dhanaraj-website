The story

Born in 1951 at Dharapuram, Dhanaraj trained as a teacher at the Government Teacher Training School there, took his M.A. in History from Madras University and his M.Ed. from Annamalai University. In 1982, a young teacher by then, he travelled to Delhi for the Centre for Cultural Resources and Training course on puppetry in education — and came home with a conviction that never left him: a puppet can teach what a lecture cannot.

He served Tamil Nadu's government schools for thirty-one years — assistant teacher from 1978, headmaster from 1988 until retirement in 2010. Through all of it, and through every year since, his evenings, holidays and retirement have belonged to the puppets.

His shows carried awareness where textbooks could not reach: child labour and child rights, women's rights and human rights, the evils of dowry, health and hygiene, rainwater harvesting, afforestation, pollution and plastic, civic values, and above all the importance of education. He performed across Tamil Nadu — district by district, taluk by taluk — alongside voluntary organisations and NGOs, and trained hundreds of teachers and child-care workers to make and use puppets themselves, so the art would not end with him.

He has never charged for a single performance. In an age when traditional arts survive by commodifying themselves, he chose the opposite path: give the art away, freely, for forty-four years, so that it lives.

The journey

Four decades on a
string and a lamp

1951
Born in Dharapuram

Born on 15 November 1951 to Mr. Nesaiah and Mrs. Thayanithi. Schooled in Udumalpet and Dharapuram, then trained as a Secondary Grade Teacher, 1969–71.

1978
A teacher's life begins

Joined Tamil Nadu's government schools as an assistant teacher — the start of thirty-one years of service. Later completed an M.A. in History at Madras University and an M.Ed. at Annamalai University.

1982
Trained at CCRT, New Delhi

Completed the Course on Puppetry for Education at the Centre for Cultural Resources and Training, and in the same year established Rainbow Puppetry — the banner under which every show since has travelled. Returned to CCRT for the refresher course in 1984.

1988
National Teacher Award

Honoured by the Government of India for valuable contribution and service to education, and appointed CCRT District Resource Person to carry puppetry into the National Policy of Education at the district level.

1989
Guest of the President

Invited by the Ministry of Defence to the Republic Day Parade in New Delhi, where the President took the salute.

1990s
The village decades

Hundreds of shows across Periyar District and beyond — schools, banks, festivals, NSS camps, women's guilds and rural development projects. Themes ranged from Ashoka's transformation to AIDS awareness and child labour, and he trained teachers and child-care workers across South India to carry the art forward.

2007
Silver Jubilee

Rainbow Puppetry marked twenty-five years — a commemorative silver jubilee of the work from 1982 to 2007. The golden jubilee now approaches in 2032.

2008
State's best teacher

Received the Dr. Radhakrishnan Award from the Government of Tamil Nadu, and the Rotary Vocational Excellence Award in the same year.

2010
Retirement — and a second career

Retired after thirty-one years as teacher and headmaster. The puppets, till then an evening's calling, became his full-time life.

2011
Eddy Trust is founded

Registered at Dharapuram in memory of his son Edwin Paul Nathaniel, the trust became the vehicle for the free shows and for supporting the education of deserving students.

2017
Honorary Doctorate in Puppetry

Conferred an honorary doctorate in Art & Culture with specialisation in Puppetry.

2023
Kalai Mudhumani

Named District Best Puppetry Artist by the Department of Art and Culture, Government of Tamil Nadu, and honoured with the Sevai Chemmal Award by the Tamil Nadu Tamil Sangam Makkal Mandram. The same year, carried his puppets to Manipur — performing at the Samathuva Pongal near the Moreh border, organised through the Tiruppur Tamil Sangam.

2025
Carrying Tamil heritage abroad

Performed internationally in Siem Reap, Cambodia, at the 1000th anniversary commemoration of Emperor Rajendra Chola's conquest of Kadaram.

Today
The work continues

Still performing, still teaching the next generation, still refusing to let a thousand-year-old art die. Recommended for the Padma Shri, 2027.

The trust behind the shows

Eddy Trust, Dharapuram

A registered charitable trust · Regd. No. 12/W/0042258 · Established 2011

The trust is named in loving memory of Edwin Paul Nathaniel — Dr. Dhanaraj's son, who was lost in an accident in the river Amaravathy. His name now travels with every free show, and with every student the trust helps to study.

Registered on 10 October 2011 at Dharapuram, Eddy Trust supports the educational needs of deserving school and college students, has provided provisions to Lister Home, Dharapuram, and carries Rainbow Puppetry's performances to audiences entirely free of cost.
